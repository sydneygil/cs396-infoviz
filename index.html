Mass Shootings in the United States from 1982 to 2021

Created by Felipe Caldeira and Sydney Gil

This interactive visualization illustrates all the cases of mass shootings in the U.S. from 1982 to 2021 (125 in total as of 12/7/2021), using data provided by MotherJones.com.
A mass shooting is defined as a single attack in a public place where four or more victims were killed (after 2013, this definition was changed to three or more victims). This dataset also includes shooting spree cases, where the killings occurred in more than one location, but still over a short period of time. Crimes primarily related to gang activity or armed robbery are not included. Hovering over data points show detailed information about the respective case, and the visualization allows for zooming and panning. Clicking on the stars next to the filter labels will color the data points according to the respective variable.
